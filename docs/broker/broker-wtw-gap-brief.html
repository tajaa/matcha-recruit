Matcha for Brokers

Every capability and gap, sourced to WTW's Insurance Marketplace Realities 2026 — what the industry asks for, why it matters, and what data we use.
The thesis. WTW's report is explicit that the modern broker competes on data, not price. WTW p.5"…your competitive edge depends on harnessing data, analytics, and technology — not just to keep pace, but to stay ahead." And carriers reward it: WTW p.4"…a buyers' market in many lines of business for risks with favorable loss history, risk mitigation plans, redundancies and quality data." That data — safety, HR, compliance — is exactly what Matcha runs. So we can hand a broker an underwriting-grade picture for free, and score a broker's off-platform book from a loss run + questionnaire. Examples below use Matcha's seeded demo book.

How the scoring works

Workers' Comp: TRIR = recordable cases × 200,000 ÷ (headcount × 2,000 hrs), banded vs the client's BLS industry benchmark (≥1.5× = critical). Plus experience-mod trajectory, cumulative-trauma/acute + post-termination split, return-to-work, and a per-state NCCI rate overlay.

EPL readiness: composite 0–100 over 10 factors (band ≥80 Strong · ≥60 Adequate · ≥35 Developing · <35 Exposed):

What we have — sourced

A · The broker cockpit HAVE

Book of Business (clients banded healthy/watch/at-risk), onboarding pipeline, seats, team, white-label, automated risk alerts + milestones, and AI outreach.

WTW p.10"Risk Intelligence Central… gives clients easy and efficient access to their risk information… so clients can optimize their risk and insurance."

Why it matters: the report's whole "tomorrow's broker" thesis is proactive, data-driven service — knowing which client to call before they do.

Have / data Built on broker_company_links (the book) + a Celery job that scans each client's incident/compliance trend into broker_risk_alerts; AI outreach is Gemini grounded in that client's own metrics.
Example Of 9 clients the strip shows 1 critical · 1 fair · 1 good; an alert fires that Bags broke its claims-free streak → broker clicks AI outreach and calls, prepared.
B · Workers' Comp depth NEW

Experience-mod trajectory, cumulative-trauma / post-termination claim taxonomy, return-to-work, and a per-state NCCI rate overlay.

WTW p.29"Willis has developed and deployed a Workers' Compensation Risk Index Model, helping clients to evaluate their year-over-year jurisdiction and hazard risk changes."  ·  p.29"…from 'windfall' to 'managed' profitability — where … claim and medical management and state-by-state execution will matter more."
WTW p.29"40% of all CT claims are filed on a post-employment or post-termination basis. These claims tend to be more litigious…"  ·  p.33"…even in states where rates are decreasing, some employers may see their experience modification factors increase."

Why it matters: the mod is the multiplier on WC premium, CT/post-term claims are the rising cost driver, and rate moves are now state-by-state — exactly what our tab surfaces.

Have / data OSHA recordables already captured in Matcha's incident product (ir_incidents: recordable flag, days-away, classification); we added claim-type + post-termination + return-to-work on each incident; EMR is broker-entered (company_wc_mods); state from business_locations; NCCI trend seeded in wc_state_rates from the report's p.32 filing table.
Example — Bags (construction · 25) TRIR 20.0 = 8× benchmark → critical; 5 CT, 2 post-term; mod 1.05→1.18→1.32; CA −2.0%. "Your premium's rising from cumulative-trauma claims, not base rates — here's the plan to bend the mod."
C · EPL readiness NEW

A 0–100 insurability score with the top gap surfaced — half from HR data, half from a quick broker checklist.

WTW p.85"Underwriters are also focusing on AI usage in the workplace, company approaches to DEI, … EEOC enforcement priorities … and compliance with state pay transparency laws. Many markets have separate questionnaires for biometrics, sexual harassment and pay equity."
WTW p.87"The risk of wage and hour claims applies to all U.S. employers. As laws … vary from state to state (and sometimes county to county), compliance has become extremely difficult for multi-state employers." (DOL recovered $184M in back wages FY2025.)

Why it matters: the report literally lists the EPL underwriting questionnaire — and Matcha already holds most of the answers, so we convert a blank application into a one-screen score.

Have / data Derived from policies/policy_signatures, training_records, progressive_discipline, er_cases, and compliance_requirements per location; the 5 items no system tracks (pay transparency, biometrics/BIPA, pay equity, AI-hiring audit, DEI) are broker-attested.
Example — Gretchin (retail · 150 · CA+IL) 69 Adequate (derived 33/55 + attested 36/45); top gaps = no harassment training, no documented discipline. "Two fixes move you to Strong — a better EPL renewal."
D · Off-platform clients — "Broker Pro" NEW

Score clients who aren't on Matcha — broker keys the carrier loss-run summary + a short EPL questionnaire; the same engines run.

WTW p.11"…if you can capture exposure data, you can help clients make a better case to the market, which drives better experiences for carriers too."

Why it matters: most of a broker's book never onboards. Capturing their exposure data is exactly the "better case to the market" the report describes — and turns our scoring engine into a standalone broker tool.

Have / data broker_external_* tables (non-tenant clients); data from the carrier loss run + an underwriting questionnaire the broker keys. Same WC math + EPL weights, so scores compare to on-platform clients.
Example — Northwind (prospect · transportation · 60 · IL) loss run: 9 recordable, 4 CT, mod 1.24, $210k → TRIR 15.0 critical, IL +1.5%, EPL 26 Exposed. Wins the account before they're even a client.

E · Off-platform v2 — no more manual keying NEW

Loss-run PDF auto-parse + a client-fills-it intake link, so the External Book scales without the data-entry tax.

WTW p.11"How do we bring data back to our brokers as quickly as possible? How do we digitize more of the value chain?"

Why it matters: the data-entry tax caps how many off-platform prospects a broker can run — removing it turns the External Book into a high-volume prospecting tool.

Have / data Carrier loss-run PDF → Gemini parse into the broker_external_wc fields (broker reviews the draft before saving). EPL answers come from a shareable intake link the prospect self-completes (public, token-gated) → writes broker_external_epl_attestations and rescoring runs automatically.
Example Broker drops a 12-page loss run → claim counts + mod prefill; texts the prospect the intake link → they answer 10 questions in 2 min → the client's EPL + risk index populate with zero keying.
F · Agentic — derive from data, don't type NEW

Like the rest of the platform (onboarding wizard, compliance research), these features now pull from data already on file + AI instead of manual entry. Manual entry stays as a fallback.

WTW p.5"…your competitive edge depends on harnessing data, analytics, and technology."  ·  p.11"…digitize more of the value chain."

Why it matters: the underwriting inputs already live in the tenant's HR/safety data — surfacing them automatically is the whole pitch. Hand-keyed registers don't scale and don't feel like the platform.

Have / data WC class-map: Gemini maps employees.job_title → NCCI class + aggregates payroll. Pay-equity: computes within-role pay dispersion from employees.pay_rate (real analysis, not a logged date). AI scan-&-suggest: proposes the AI-hiring-tool / biometric / safety-program registers from industry + roles + incident history. Risk narrative: AI "why your index is X + top moves" over the composite score.
Example — Sea Cafe One click → 17 employees mapped to class 9082 ($836k payroll); pay-equity flags Line Cook 34.8% + GM 33.1% spread; suggests fingerprint time-clocks + slip-and-fall program; narrative cites the 92 WC / 69 EPL scores with prioritized fixes.

Licensed per-class NCCI base rates · DEI posture as a tool

WTW p.85"…separate questionnaires for biometrics, sexual harassment and pay equity."  ·  p.32 class-level rate detail.

Plugged since the last review: real public state loss-cost trends loaded (NCCI 2026 filings); an admin CSV import for rate data; pay-equity upgraded from a logged date to a real payroll analysis; and the registers (class-codes, AI-tools, biometric, safety programs) now auto-derive from employees + AI. What's left is genuinely small:

Data path Per-class NCCI base rates — the only truly licensed piece; import pipeline is live at /admin/wc-rate-data, just needs a purchased manual uploaded (public state trends already in). DEI → workforce demographics (attested today; a real analysis needs gender/race data not in our schema, i.e. an HRIS /individual pull) — same gap that limits pay-equity to a within-role dispersion screen rather than a protected-class gap.
