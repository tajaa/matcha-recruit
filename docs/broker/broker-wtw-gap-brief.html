Matcha for Brokers

How it works, with worked examples — and what's still missing. Mapped to WTW's Insurance Marketplace Realities 2026.
The opportunity. WTW's 2026 report says brokers win not by shopping policies but by turning a client's data into a credible risk story that earns better terms at renewal. That data — workplace safety, HR practices, compliance — is exactly what Matcha runs. So Matcha can hand a broker an underwriting-grade risk picture for clients on the platform, and run the same scoring on a broker's off-platform book from a loss run + a questionnaire. The rest of this brief shows the mechanics and walks real examples (using our seeded demo book). All numbers below are from the live demo data, not illustrations.

How the scoring works (the mechanics)

Workers' Comp. From a client's recordable injuries we compute the same metrics carriers price on:

The math
TRIR (injury rate) = recordable cases × 200,000 ÷ (headcount × 2,000 hrs). We band it against the client's industry benchmark (BLS): below 0.75× = good, up to 1.5× = at-risk, ≥1.5× = critical. We also track the experience mod (EMR) over time, split claims into cumulative-trauma vs acute + a post-termination flag, measure return-to-work, and overlay the state's NCCI rate trend.

EPL readiness. A composite 0–100 score over 10 factors — 55% computed from HR data Matcha already holds, 45% broker-attested from the underwriting questionnaire. Band: ≥80 Strong · ≥60 Adequate · ≥35 Developing · <35 Exposed.

What we have today — with examples

A · Run the whole book — the cockpit HAVE

Book of Business dashboard (every client banded healthy/watch/at-risk), onboarding pipeline, seats + referral links, multi-user team with roles, white-label branding, automated risk alerts + milestones, and AI outreach talking points grounded in each client's own data.

Example — Monday morning
The broker opens Book of Business. Of 9 clients, the strip shows 1 critical · 1 fair · 1 good and $— net premium exposure. An alert fires: Bags broke a claims-free streak. The broker clicks AI outreach → gets a drafted talking-track citing Bags' own numbers, and calls them — before the client even knows there's a problem.
B · Workers' Comp depth NEW

Experience-mod trajectory, cumulative-trauma / post-termination claim taxonomy, return-to-work, and a per-state NCCI rate overlay — on the client-detail "Workers' Comp" tab.

Example — Bags (construction · 25 employees · California)
The tab shows TRIR 20.0 — 8× the construction benchmark (~2.5) → critical. Claim mix: 5 cumulative-trauma, 2 filed post-termination, 3 still-open lost-time. Experience mod climbing 1.05 → 1.18 → 1.32 (a 32% surcharge). California loss-costs −2.0%. "Your premium is rising because of cumulative-trauma claims — not base rates, which are actually down 2%. Here's an ergonomics + return-to-work plan to bend the mod before renewal."
C · EPL readiness NEW

A 0–100 insurability score per client, with the single biggest gap surfaced — half from HR data, half from a quick broker checklist.

Example — Gretchin Weiners (retail · 150 employees · CA + IL)
Score 69 — Adequate (derived 33/55 + attested 36/45). Strong points: anti-harassment policy in place with 92% signed; pay-transparency & DEI attested in place. Top gaps: no anti-harassment training and no documented discipline process. "Two fixes — roll out harassment training and document your discipline steps — move you from Adequate to Strong, and that's a better EPL renewal."
D · Off-platform clients — "Broker Pro" NEW

Score clients who aren't on Matcha. Admin flips the broker to the Pro plan; an "External Book" appears. The broker adds a non-tenant client, keys the carrier loss-run summary + a short EPL questionnaire, and the same engines score them.

Example — Northwind Trucking (prospect, not a Matcha client · transportation · 60 employees · IL)
Broker pastes the loss run: 9 recordables, 5 DART, 4 cumulative-trauma, 1 post-term, mod 1.24, premium $210k. Instantly: TRIR 15.0 → critical, IL rates +1.5%, and a questionnaire-driven EPL 26 — Exposed. "You're a prospect, not even my client yet — but here's exactly where your WC and EPL risk sits and the plan to fix it before your renewal." That's how the broker wins the account.

What's still missing — and why, with examples

✦ Biggest remaining — the terms-winning layer (the report's actual thesis)

Carrier-ready submission packet + AI coverage-gap analysis

We hold the risk picture inward (dashboards + WC/EPL scores). We don't yet package it outward for carriers, or flag where a client is under-covered.
Today: the broker re-types Bags' TRIR, mod history (1.05→1.32), claim mix and loss-mitigation notes into a Word doc, then emails 6 carriers individually.
With it: one click → a branded PDF submission per carrier from the data already on file, plus an AI flag like "Bags carries $1M EPL; a 25-person construction crew with 2 post-term CT claims supports $2M — and there's no cyber despite employee PII."
Why it's #1: this is what literally wins better pricing at renewal, and it sits directly on top of the scoring we just shipped.
✦ Off-platform v2 — remove the manual keying

Loss-run PDF auto-parse + a client-fills-it intake link

Today the broker hand-keys the Northwind loss-run figures and questionnaire.
Today: broker reads a 12-page carrier PDF and types 14 numbers per prospect; the EPL answers require a separate call with the client.
With it: drop the PDF → fields auto-fill; send the client a link they complete themselves (no onboarding) → the book scales from a handful of prospects to dozens.
✦ Year-round client value + one number

Client-facing risk portal + a single composite risk index

Everything is broker-only today, and risk lives in three separate scores (WC band, EPL number, compliance).
With it: Bags' owner logs into a portal and sees "Your risk index: 41/100 (peer avg 68). Top 3 fixes: ergonomics program, return-to-work policy, document discipline." — one benchmarkable number, and the broker looks proactive every month, not just at renewal.
✦ Depth where it's currently thin

EPL factors as real tools · full WC data · healthcare vertical

EPL "attested" → tracked tools: pay transparency, BIPA, pay equity, AI-hiring audits, DEI are broker yes/no/partial today; e.g. a real pay-transparency checker that scans the client's job postings for the 5 states that require salary ranges. WC data: only the report's named states are seeded — needs full 50-state NCCI rates + class codes (so a Texas or Ohio client isn't "no data"). Healthcare: the report calls a strong resident-care program "a valuable asset to highlight for insurers" — package credentialing + incidents + fall-prevention for senior-living PL.

Why this matters & what's next

The bet: Matcha is the only HR platform that already sits on the exact data carriers underwrite on — and now scores it for on- and off-platform clients alike. Each remaining step turns more of that data into something a broker can sell with.

  • Outward submission packet + AI coverage-gap — the terms-winning thesis; sits right on top of the scoring already shipped.
  • Off-platform v2 — loss-run PDF auto-parse + client intake link, so the External Book scales without manual keying.
  • Client portal + composite risk index — year-round client value + one benchmarkable number.
  • Vertical + data depth — healthcare resident-care asset, full state WC rates, EPL trackers.
