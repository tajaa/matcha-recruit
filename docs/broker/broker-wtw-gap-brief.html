PRODUCT BRIEF · 2026-06-20

Matcha for Brokers

What the broker product does today, and what's still missing — mapped to WTW's Insurance Marketplace Realities 2026.
The opportunity. WTW's 2026 market report says the same thing on every employer-facing line: brokers win not by shopping policies, but by turning a client's data into a credible risk story that earns better terms at renewal. That data is workplace safety, HR practices, and compliance — exactly the systems Matcha already runs. Two consequences: (1) for clients on Matcha, the broker gets an underwriting-grade risk picture for free; (2) Matcha can run the same scoring on a broker's off-platform book from a loss run + a questionnaire. We've built the depth (Workers' Comp + EPL) and the off-platform reach (Broker Pro).

What we have today

A · Run the whole book — the broker cockpit HAVE
Book of Business dashboard. Every client with risk signals (compliance %, open actions, safety) banded healthy / watch / at-risk. Helps: see who needs attention without opening 50 accounts.
Onboarding + pipeline. Create a client setup, pre-configure their features, send the invite, track submitted → live. Helps: standardized, trackable client setup.
Seats & referral links. Allocate licenses across the book; shareable Matcha-Lite signup links that auto-attribute. Helps: grow the book with low friction.
Team, white-label & risk intel. Multi-user broker org with roles; your logo/colors/login domain; automated risk-trend alerts + positive milestones; AI outreach talking points grounded in each client's own data. Helps: run a branded brokerage and show up to renewals prepared.
B · Workers' Comp depth NEW
Experience-mod (EMR) trajectory. Track the client's mod over time, with carrier + premium. Why: the mod is the single number carriers multiply WC premium by — the report stresses it can rise even when base rates fall.
Claim taxonomy. Recordables split into cumulative-trauma vs one-time injuries, plus a post-termination flag. Why: the report names CT + post-term claims the fastest-rising, most-litigated cost driver.
Return-to-work + NCCI state overlay. Open vs resolved lost-time and avg days back; each operating state's WC rate trend. Helps: explain exactly why a premium is moving and what to fix, by jurisdiction.
C · EPL readiness NEW
A 0–100 insurability score per client with a band (Strong → Exposed) and the single biggest gap. Helps: a one-screen "are they placeable, and what to fix" instead of a blank carrier application.
~Half computed from HR data Matcha already holds — anti-harassment policy + sign-off, training completion, documented discipline, ER-case handling, multi-state wage & hour. The rest is a quick broker checklist for what no system tracks (pay transparency, biometrics/BIPA, pay equity, AI-hiring audits, DEI). Why: these are exactly the questions the report shows EPL underwriters now ask.
D · Off-platform clients — "Broker Pro" NEW
Score clients who aren't on Matcha. Flip a broker to the Pro plan (admin toggle) and they get an "External Book": add a non-tenant client, key in the carrier loss-run summary + a short EPL questionnaire, and the same WC + EPL engines score them — directly comparable to on-platform clients. Why it's the unlock: most of a broker's book never onboards. This turns the scoring engine from a pass-through add-on into a tool they use for every prospect and renewal.

What's still missing — and why we need it

✦ Biggest remaining — the terms-winning layer (the report's actual thesis)

Turn our risk picture into a carrier-ready submission + coverage-gap analysis

We now hold the risk picture inward (broker dashboards + WC/EPL scores, on- and off-platform). We don't yet package it outward: a carrier-ready underwriting submission generated from that posture, and an AI coverage-gap analysis that flags where a client is under-protected. Why we need it: this is the literal thesis of the WTW report and what actually wins better pricing at renewal. Until we build it, brokers still hand-assemble submissions in Word — Matcha informs the conversation but doesn't close it. This is the highest-leverage next step because it sits directly on top of the scoring we just shipped.
✦ Off-platform v2 — remove the manual keying

Loss-run PDF auto-parse + a client-fills-it intake link

Today the broker keys the loss-run figures and questionnaire by hand. Why we need it: loss runs arrive as messy carrier PDFs and the underwriting answers live with the client. Auto-parsing the PDF and letting the company complete a shareable intake link (without ever onboarding) removes the data-entry tax and makes the off-platform book scale to dozens of prospects at once.
✦ Client-facing value + a single number

A self-serve client risk portal + one composite risk index

Everything is broker-only today. A client-facing portal ("here's your risk and how to improve your terms") makes the broker stickier and gives the client a reason to engage year-round. A single composite risk index (rolling WC + EPL + compliance into one score, the report's "risk index model" idea) gives an at-a-glance number to benchmark and trend. Why: differentiation and retention — the client sees the broker's value continuously, not just at renewal.
✦ Depth where it's currently thin

EPL factors as real tools · full WC data · healthcare vertical

EPL "attested" → tracked tools: pay transparency, BIPA, pay equity, AI-hiring audits and DEI are broker yes/no/partial today; each could become an actual tracker if EPL becomes a headline product. WC data completeness: full 50-state NCCI rate coverage + class-code detail (only the report's named states are seeded). Healthcare resident-care asset: the report calls a strong resident-care program "a valuable asset to highlight for insurers" — packaging credentialing + incidents + safety programs for senior-living/healthcare PL leans into Matcha's vertical strength. Why: these turn "good enough" areas into clear differentiators.

Why this matters & what's next

The bet: Matcha is the only HR platform that already sits on the exact data carriers underwrite on — and now scores it for both on- and off-platform clients. Every remaining step turns more of that data into something a broker can sell with. Suggested order:

  • Outward submission packet + AI coverage-gap — the terms-winning thesis; sits right on top of the scoring already shipped.
  • Off-platform v2 — loss-run PDF auto-parse + client intake link, so the External Book scales without manual keying.
  • Client portal + composite risk index — year-round client value + a single benchmarkable number.
  • Vertical + data depth — healthcare resident-care asset, full state WC rates, EPL trackers.
