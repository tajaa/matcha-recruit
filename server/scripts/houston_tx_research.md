## General Labor

### minimum_wage

*Federal only — no state-specific requirements beyond FLSA.* Texas adopts the federal minimum wage of $7.25/hr by reference. State law specifically prohibits cities from establishing local minimum wage rates. No tipped wage differential beyond federal $2.13/hr.

### overtime

*Federal only — no state-specific requirements beyond FLSA.* Texas has no daily overtime, no double time, no seventh-day overtime rules. Standard federal 40hrs/week threshold at 1.5x applies. State employees have a comp-time option, but private sector follows FLSA only.

### sick_leave

*Federal only — no state or city paid sick leave law.* Texas has no state-mandated paid sick leave. Local ordinance attempts in Austin, Dallas, and San Antonio were blocked by courts. Houston has no local ordinance.

## Healthcare

### hipaa_privacy

#### State Health Privacy Laws
- **regulation_key**: `state_health_privacy_laws`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: Texas
- **title**: Texas Medical Records Privacy Act (TMRPA) & HB 300
- **description**: Texas HB 300 (2011) updated the Texas Health & Safety Code Chapter 181 with protections exceeding HIPAA. Key differences: (1) Broader coverage — applies to any entity possessing PHI including sports teams, IT providers, lawyers, accountants (not just HIPAA covered entities). (2) Breach notification within 60 days, with AG notification within 30 days if 250+ residents affected. (3) Extraterritorial application — extends to organizations outside Texas handling Texans' health data. (4) Employee training on state privacy requirements mandated for covered entities. Additional layers: Texas Identity Theft Enforcement and Protection Act, Texas Data Privacy and Security Act, SB 1188 (2023).
- **current_value**: TMRPA/HB 300 — broader than HIPAA; 60-day breach notification; AG notice at 250+ affected
- **effective_date**: 2012-09-01
- **source_url**: https://www.hipaajournal.com/what-is-texas-hb-300/
- **source_name**: HIPAA Journal — Texas HB 300
- **requires_written_policy**: true

### billing_integrity

#### State False Claims Acts
- **regulation_key**: `state_false_claims_acts`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: Texas
- **title**: Texas Medicaid Fraud Prevention Act (TMFPA)
- **description**: Texas passed its own False Claims Act in 1995 targeting Medicaid fraud specifically. Includes qui tam (whistleblower) provisions — whistleblowers receive 15-25% if state intervenes, 25-30% if not. Civil penalties $5,500-$11,000 per violation plus double damages. SB 1038 (effective Sept 2025) elevated unknowing violations to same administrative penalty level as intentional fraud — up to $10,000/violation plus treble damages.
- **current_value**: TMFPA — qui tam provisions; $5,500-$11,000 per violation; treble damages (SB 1038)

- **regulation_key**: `state_radiation_control_programs`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: Texas
- **title**: Texas Radiation Control Program — NRC Agreement State
- **description**: Texas became an NRC Agreement State in 1963. The Department of State Health Services (DSHS) is the Texas Radiation Control Agency under Health & Safety Code Chapter 401. Program covers ~25% radioactive materials permits and ~75% X-ray/mammography/laser permits. DSHS and TCEQ coordinate rulemaking to ensure NRC compatibility. DSHS licenses, inspects, and enforces for medical, academic, research, and industrial uses of radioactive materials.
- **current_value**: NRC Agreement State since 1963; DSHS administers

### tumor_registry

- **regulation_key**: `tx_cancer_incidence_reporting_act`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: Texas
- **title**: Texas Cancer Incidence Reporting Act
- **description**: Mandatory cancer reporting under Texas Health & Safety Code Chapter 82. All hospitals, cancer centers, ambulatory surgical centers, clinical pathology labs, and in some cases physicians/dentists must report. Deadline: within 6 months of initial diagnosis or admission. Facilities with >400 annual caseload must submit monthly; ≤400 at least quarterly. Weekly submission recommended.
