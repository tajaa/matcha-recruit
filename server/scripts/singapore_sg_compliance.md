## 1. `minimum_wage` — Minimum Wage

### Progressive Wage Model (No Universal Minimum Wage)
- **Regulation Key**: `national_minimum_wage`
- **Description**: Singapore has NO universal minimum wage. Instead, the Progressive Wage Model (PWM) mandates minimum wages for 9 specific sectors/occupations covering 155,000+ local workers (Singapore Citizens and PRs only). Sectors: cleaning, security, landscape, retail, food services, waste management, administrators, drivers. Additionally, the Local Qualifying Salary (LQS) of S$1,600/month (rising to S$1,800 from 1 Jul 2026) sets a floor for local workers to count toward foreign worker quotas.
- **Current Value**: No universal minimum; PWM sector rates from S$1,740-S$2,795/month; LQS S$1,600/month

## 2. `overtime` — Overtime

### Singapore Overtime Pay
- **Regulation Key**: `daily_weekly_overtime`
- **Description**: Employment Act Part IV mandates 1.5x hourly basic rate for all overtime. Only applies to workmen earning ≤S$4,500/month and non-workmen earning ≤S$2,600/month. Managers and executives are NOT covered. Maximum 72 hours OT per month (can only be exceeded with MOM Commissioner approval). Standard hours: 44 hours/week (or 48 with 2-week averaging), 8 hours/day (6-day week) or 9 hours/day (5-day week). OT pay for non-workmen capped at salary level of S$2,600 (hourly rate of S$13.60). Formula: (Monthly basic salary x 12) / (52 x 44).
- **Current Value**: 1.5x hourly basic rate; max 72 hours OT/month
- **Numeric Value**: 1.5
- **Jurisdiction Level**: national
- **Jurisdiction Name**: Singapore
- **Requires Written Policy**: False
- **Source URL**: https://www.mom.gov.sg/employment-practices/hours-of-work-overtime-and-rest-days
- **Source Name**: Ministry of Manpower (MOM)

### Maximum Working Hours
- **Regulation Key**: `mandatory_overtime_restrictions`
- **Description**: Employment Act Part IV: maximum 44 hours/week standard, or 48 hours/week with 2-week averaging (88 hours over 2 consecutive weeks). Maximum 8 hours/day for 6-day weeks, 9 hours/day for 5-day weeks. Overtime capped at 72 hours/month — exceeding requires prior written approval from MOM Commissioner. Only applies to Part IV employees (workmen ≤S$4,500, non-workmen ≤S$2,600). Rest day: 1 per week mandatory (24 consecutive hours). Working on rest day at employer's request: 2x basic rate (full day) or 1x basic rate (half day or less).
- **Current Value**: 44 hours/week standard; 72 hours OT/month max

## 3. `sick_leave` — Sick Leave

### Statutory Sick Leave
- **Regulation Key**: `statutory_sick_leave`
- **Description**: Employment Act provides paid sick leave after 3 months of service. Full entitlement (6+ months): 14 days outpatient + 60 days hospitalisation (inclusive of 14 outpatient days) per year. Pro-ration: 3 months service = 5 outpatient/15 hospitalisation days; 4 months = 8/30; 5 months = 11/45. Must be certified by company-approved or government doctor. Employee must notify employer within 48 hours. Employer pays full salary for sick leave days.
- **Current Value**: 14 days outpatient + 60 days hospitalisation per year (at full entitlement)
- **Numeric Value**: 14
- **Jurisdiction Level**: national
- **Jurisdiction Name**: Singapore
- **Requires Written Policy**: False

## 5. `meal_breaks` — Meal & Rest Breaks

### Meal Break (Part IV Employees)
- **Regulation Key**: `meal_break`
- **Description**: Employment Act Part IV: employees must not work more than 6 consecutive hours without a break. Break must be at least 45 minutes. Breaks are generally unpaid unless contract states otherwise. Break time not counted as working hours. Only applies to Part IV employees (workmen ≤S$4,500, non-workmen ≤S$2,600). Managers/executives and higher earners: break entitlements governed solely by employment contract. Practical norm: most employers provide 1-hour lunch break for standard 9-hour workday (8 hours actual work).
- **Current Value**: 45-minute break per 6 consecutive hours of work

## 7. `pay_frequency` — Pay Frequency

### Salary Payment Period
- **Regulation Key**: `standard_pay_frequency`
- **Description**: Employment Act Part III: salary period must not exceed 1 month. Salary must be paid within 7 days after end of salary period. Overtime pay within 14 days. Employer and employee may agree on shorter period (weekly, bi-weekly). Soft or hard copy acceptable. Records retained at least 2 years (current employees) and 2 years after departure. Penalty for no payslip: S$2,000 fine (first offence), S$4,000 (subsequent).

- **Jurisdiction Name**: Singapore

## 9. `workers_comp` — Workers' Comp

- **Regulation Key**: `mandatory_coverage`
- **Description**: Work Injury Compensation Act 2019. No-fault compensation for all employees regardless of salary, age, or citizenship. ## 11. `anti_discrimination` — Anti-Discrimination

### Workplace Fairness Act 2025
- **Regulation Key**: `protected_classes`
- **Description**: Workplace Fairness Act 2025 — Singapore's first comprehensive anti-discrimination law. Passed 8 January 2025, assented 3 February 2025. Expected in force by end of 2027. Previously relied on voluntary TAFEP Tripartite Guidelines. 11 protected characteristics across 5 categories: age, nationality, sex (assigned at birth or reassigned — does NOT include sexual orientation/gender identity), marital status, pregnancy status (including intent to bear children/breastfeeding), caregiving responsibilities, race, religion, language ability, disability, mental health conditions. Applies to employers with 25+ employees (smaller employers exempt for first 5 years except for fair consideration of job applicants). Penalties: S$5,000 first contravention, S$10,000 subsequent; serious breaches up to S$50,000 initially, S$250,000 repeat. TAFEP investigates complaints.
- **Current Value**: 11 protected characteristics; applies to 25+ employee firms
- **Numeric Value**: 25
- **Jurisdiction Level**: national
- **Jurisdiction Name**: Singapore
- **Requires Written Policy**: False
- **Effective Date**: 2027-12-31

### Hour Limits for Young Workers
- **Regulation Key**: `hour_limits_14_15`
- **Description**: Children (under 15, still in school): max 6 hours/day. Children must receive 30-minute break every 3 hours. Young persons (15-16, still in school): max 7 hours/day, 30-minute break every 4 hours. Night work prohibited for under-16 workmen (11pm-6am). Employment Act Part IV limits for young persons covered under it: same as adult Part IV limits (44 hrs/week, 8/9 hrs/day).
- **Current Value**: Under 15: max 6 hrs/day; 15-16: max 7 hrs/day
- **Numeric Value**: 6
- **Jurisdiction Level**: national
- **Jurisdiction Name**: Singapore
- **Requires Written Policy**: False
- **Source URL**: https://www.mom.gov.sg/employment-practices/young-persons-and-children
- **Source Name**: Ministry of Manpower (MOM)

## 13. `sg_specific` — Singapore-Specific Provisions

### Foreign Worker Levy
- **Regulation Key**: `sg_foreign_worker_levy`
- **Description**: Employers of foreign workers must pay monthly levies. S Pass: harmonized at S$650/month from 1 Sep 2025. Work Permit levies vary by sector: Manufacturing Tier 1 R1 S$330, R2 S$650; Services Tier 1 R1 S$450, R2 S$650; Construction R1 S$750, R2 S$950. R1 = higher skilled, R2 = basic skilled. Dependency ratio ceilings limit foreign worker proportion (e.g., services ~35%). Marine/Process sectors seeing R2 levy increases. Tier restructuring merging Tier 1 and Tier 2 underway.
- **Current Value**: S$650/month S Pass; Work Permit varies S$330-S$950 by sector/skill
- **Numeric Value**: 650
- **Jurisdiction Level**: national
- **Jurisdiction Name**: Singapore
- **Requires Written Policy**: False
- **Effective Date**: 2025-09-01
- **Source URL**: https://www.mom.gov.sg/passes-and-permits/work-permit-for-foreign-worker/foreign-worker-levy
- **Source Name**: Ministry of Manpower (MOM)

### Skills Development Levy (SDL)
- **Regulation Key**: `sg_skills_development_levy`
- **Description**: Skills Development Levy Act. All employers must pay 0.25% of monthly total wages for all employees (including foreign employees). Minimum S$2 per employee (for those earning <S$800/month). Maximum S$11.25 per employee (for those earning >S$4,500/month). Collected by CPF Board on behalf of SkillsFuture Singapore Agency. Funds SkillsFuture initiatives and workforce upgrading programmes.
- **Current Value**: 0.25% of wages (min S$2, max S$11.25 per employee)
- **Numeric Value**: 0.25
- **Jurisdiction Level**: national
- **Jurisdiction Name**: Singapore
- **Requires Written Policy**: False
- **Source URL**: https://www.cpf.gov.sg/employer/employer-obligations/skills-development-levy
- **Source Name**: CPF Board

### Retirement and Re-employment
- **Regulation Key**: `sg_retirement_reemployment`
- **Description**: Retirement and Re-employment Act 1993. Current minimum retirement age: 63 (from Jul 2022). Re-employment obligation: up to age 68. FROM 1 JULY 2026: retirement age rises to 64 (born on/after 1 Jul 1963); re-employment age rises to 69 (born on/after 1 Jul 1958). Government target by 2030: retirement 65, re-employment 70. Employer must offer re-employment to eligible employees (SG citizen/PR, satisfactory performance, medically fit, 2+ years service). Must begin discussions 6 months before employee turns retirement age. Re-employment contract minimum 1 year, renewable annually.
- **Current Value**: Retirement age 63 (64 from Jul 2026); re-employment 68 (69 from Jul 2026)
- **Numeric Value**: 63
- **Jurisdiction Level**: national
- **Jurisdiction Name**: Singapore
- **Requires Written Policy**: False
- **Effective Date**: 2022-07-01
- **Source URL**: https://www.mom.gov.sg/employment-practices/retirement
- **Source Name**: Ministry of Manpower (MOM)

### Employment Act Coverage Scope
- **Regulation Key**: `sg_employment_act_coverage`
- **Description**: Employment Act 1968 covers all employees (local and foreign) under a contract of service, including full-time, part-time, temporary, and contract staff. Excluded entirely: seafarers (Merchant Shipping Act), domestic workers, statutory board employees, civil servants. Part IV (hours/OT/rest days) only applies to workmen ≤S$4,500/month and non-workmen ≤S$2,600/month. Managers and executives are NOT covered under Part IV regardless of salary. General provisions (salary payment, sick leave, annual leave, public holidays, wrongful dismissal) apply to ALL covered employees regardless of salary.
- **Current Value**: All employees except seafarers, domestic workers, stat board, civil servants
- **Jurisdiction Level**: national
- **Jurisdiction Name**: Singapore
- **Requires Written Policy**: False
- **Source URL**: https://www.mom.gov.sg/employment-practices/employment-act/who-is-covered
- **Source Name**: Ministry of Manpower (MOM)
