### minimum_wage

#### National General Minimum Wage
- **regulation_key**: `national_minimum_wage`
- **jurisdiction_level**: national
- **jurisdiction_name**: Mexico
- **title**: Mexico National General Minimum Wage
- **description**: Mexico's national general minimum wage (salario mínimo general) is set annually by CONASAMI. Effective January 1, 2026, the daily minimum wage is MXN $315.04 (approximately MXN $9,582.47/month based on 30.4 days). This represents a 13% increase over 2025 (MXN $278.80/day), composed of a 6.5% percentage increase plus a Monto Independiente de Recuperación (MIR) of MXN $17.01. Under LFT Article 90, the minimum wage must be sufficient to satisfy the normal material, social, and cultural needs of the head of a family and to provide for the compulsory education of children.
- **current_value**: MXN $315.04/day; MXN $9,582.47/month
- **numeric_value**: 315.04
- **effective_date**: 2026-01-01
- **source_url**: https://www.gob.mx/conasami
- **source_name**: CONASAMI / Comisión Nacional de los Salarios Mínimos

### overtime

#### Overtime Pay Rates (Horas Extraordinarias)
- **regulation_key**: `daily_weekly_overtime`
- **jurisdiction_level**: national
- **jurisdiction_name**: Mexico
- **title**: Mexico Overtime Pay Rates (LFT Articles 66-68)
- **description**: Standard workday is 8 hours (day shift), 7 hours (night shift), or 7.5 hours (mixed shift). Overtime is capped at 3 hours per day, no more than 3 times per week (9 hours/week max). First 9 hours of weekly overtime are paid at double the normal hourly rate (200%). Any overtime beyond 9 hours/week must be paid at triple the normal hourly rate (300%). Overtime must be voluntary and occasional; employers cannot make it a permanent condition.
- **current_value**: Max 3 hrs/day, 3 days/week (9 hrs/week); first 9 hrs at 2x; excess at 3x
- **numeric_value**: 9
- **effective_date**: 1970-04-01

### sick_leave

#### Statutory Sick Leave (Employer Obligations)
- **regulation_key**: `statutory_sick_leave`
- **jurisdiction_level**: national
- **jurisdiction_name**: Mexico
- **title**: Mexico Statutory Sick Leave — Employer Obligations
- **description**: Mexico does not have a separate employer-paid sick leave statute in the LFT. Sick leave is managed through the IMSS social security system under the Ley del Seguro Social (LSS). When a worker is medically certified as unable to work due to non-work-related illness (enfermedad general), the employer is not required to pay wages but must maintain the employment relationship and cannot terminate the worker for being ill. The employer's obligation is to register employees with IMSS and pay contributions.
- **current_value**: Employer must maintain employment during IMSS-certified illness; no statutory employer-paid sick leave
- **effective_date**: 1997-01-01
- **source_url**: https://www.imss.gob.mx/
- **source_name**: Instituto Mexicano del Seguro Social (IMSS)
- **requires_written_policy**: false

#### IMSS Sick Leave Benefits (Subsidio por Incapacidad)
- **regulation_key**: `imss_sick_leave`
- **jurisdiction_level**: national
- **jurisdiction_name**: Mexico
- **title**: IMSS Sick Leave Cash Benefits (LSS Articles 96-98)
- **description**: IMSS pays a subsidio (cash benefit) to workers incapacitated due to non-work-related illness. Requirements: worker must have at least 4 weeks of IMSS contributions in the prior 6 months. Payment begins on the 4th day of incapacity (first 3 days are unpaid waiting period). The benefit is 60% of the worker's registered daily wage with IMSS (salario base de cotización). Maximum duration: 52 weeks. If illness persists, IMSS may extend for an additional 26 weeks (total 78 weeks) if prognosis indicates recovery. For work-related injuries/occupational diseases (riesgo de trabajo), benefits are 100% of salary from day 1 under LSS Articles 58-62.
- **current_value**: 60% of IMSS-registered salary from day 4; up to 52 weeks (extendable to 78)
- **numeric_value**: 60
- **paid**: true
- **max_weeks**: 52
- **effective_date**: 1997-01-01
- **source_url**: https://www.imss.gob.mx/
- **source_name**: Ley del Seguro Social, Articles 96-98
- **requires_written_policy**: false

#### Profit Sharing (PTU)
- **regulation_key**: `ptu_profit_sharing`
- **jurisdiction_level**: national
- **jurisdiction_name**: Mexico
- **title**: Mexico PTU / Profit Sharing (Constitutional Art. 123-A IX; LFT Arts. 117-131)
- **description**: Employers must distribute 10% of annual pre-tax profits to employees. Due by May 30 each year (60 days after annual tax return). Eligible employees must have worked at least 60 days during the fiscal year. Distribution: 50% equally among all eligible employees, 50% proportional to each worker's total wages. Per LFT Art. 127 VIII (2021 outsourcing reform, upheld by Supreme Court April 2024), PTU is capped at the greater of: (a) 3 months of the employee's salary, or (b) the average PTU received in the prior 3 years. Exempt: newly created companies (first year), IMSS, and companies with less than MXN $300,000 in taxable income.
- **current_value**: 10% of pre-tax profits; capped at 3 months' salary or 3-year average; due by May 30
- **numeric_value**: 10
- **paid**: true
- **effective_date**: 2021-04-24

#### Severance Pay (Indemnización por Despido Injustificado)
- **regulation_key**: `severance_pay`
- **jurisdiction_level**: national
- **jurisdiction_name**: Mexico
- **title**: Mexico Severance Pay — Unjust Dismissal (LFT Arts. 48-50, 89)
- **description**: When an employer terminates without just cause (despido injustificado), the worker is entitled to: (1) Constitutional indemnity: 3 months of integrated daily salary (SDI); (2) 20 days of SDI per year of service (in lieu of reinstatement, per Art. 50); (3) Seniority premium: 12 days of salary per year of service, capped at 2x daily minimum wage; (4) Proportional accrued benefits: unpaid wages, proportional vacation, vacation premium, and proportional aguinaldo. The SDI includes base salary plus all regular benefits prorated daily. If worker elects reinstatement, employer must also pay back wages (salarios caídos) capped at 12 months plus 2% monthly interest thereafter. Mexico does not recognize at-will employment.
- **current_value**: 3 months SDI + 20 days SDI/year of service + seniority premium + accrued benefits
- **effective_date**: 2012-11-30
- **source_url**: https://www.diputados.gob.mx/LeyesBiblio/pdf/LFT.pdf
- **source_name**: Ley Federal del Trabajo, Articles 48-50, 89

#### Rest Break / Ley Silla (Seating and Rest Requirements)
- **regulation_key**: `rest_break`
- **jurisdiction_level**: national
- **jurisdiction_name**: Mexico
- **title**: Mexico Ley Silla — Seating and Rest Requirements (2024 Reform)
- **description**: The Ley Silla reform (published December 19, 2024; effective June 17, 2025) amended LFT Articles 132, 133, 422, and 423 to require employers to provide sufficient chairs/seats with backrests for all employees, both for performing duties and for periodic rest. ### final_pay

- **description**: Oncology clinical trials follow the standard COFEPRIS authorization framework with additional reliance pathways for expedited review. #### Ley de Voluntad Anticipada — Advance Directives (CDMX)
- **regulation_key**: `palliative_care_access`
- **jurisdiction_level**: city
- **jurisdiction_name**: Mexico City
- **title**: Mexico City Ley de Voluntad Anticipada — Advance Directives & Palliative Care
- **description**: Mexico City's Ley de Voluntad Anticipada (enacted January 7, 2008) grants terminally ill patients the right to refuse or withdraw life-sustaining treatment (passive euthanasia). Any adult may sign an advance directive before a notary public or through authorized health institutions, designating representatives and expressing preferences for end-of-life care and organ donation. Requires diagnosis of terminal, incurable, irreversible illness with < 6 months to live. As of 2024, similar laws enacted in 16+ Mexican states. At the federal level, Ley General de Salud was amended to include palliative care provisions.
