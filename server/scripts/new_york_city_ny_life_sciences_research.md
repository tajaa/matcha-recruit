### gmp_manufacturing

#### NY State Board of Pharmacy Manufacturer Establishment Registration
- **regulation_key**: `cgmp_drugs_21cfr210_211`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: New York
- **title**: NY State Board of Pharmacy Manufacturer Establishment Registration
- **description**: Under NY Education Law Article 137 and 8 NYCRR 63.6, all drug manufacturers operating in or shipping into New York must register with the NY State Board of Pharmacy (NYSED Office of Professions). Requirements go beyond federal FDA facility registration: (1) a registered pharmacist or qualified individual with a bachelor's degree in chemistry/pharmaceutical manufacturing/biochemistry/microbiology and 2+ years experience must be present at all times the facility is open; (2) the registered area must measure no less than 300 square feet and not be shared with any other business; (3) records of receipt and disposition of all drugs/devices must be maintained for 5 years; (4) the Board of Pharmacy must be notified within 30 days of any change in corporate officers or stockholders holding 10%+ of stock; (5) out-of-state manufacturers must also register ($825 fee) with notarized application, home state license copy, and license verification.
- **effective_date**: Ongoing
- **source_url**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/new-york/8-NYCRR-63.6
- **source_name**: 8 NYCRR 63.6 - Registration and operation of New York establishments
- **requires_written_policy**: false

#### NY DOH Bureau of Narcotic Enforcement Controlled Substance Manufacturer License
- **regulation_key**: `ny_controlled_substance_manufacturer_license`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: New York
- **title**: NY DOH Bureau of Narcotic Enforcement Controlled Substance Manufacturer License
- **description**: Under NY Public Health Law Article 33 and 10 NYCRR Part 80, any person manufacturing controlled substances in New York must obtain a separate license from the NYS Department of Health Bureau of Narcotic Enforcement (BNE), in addition to federal DEA registration. Requirements include: (1) a full-time pharmacist; (2) the responsible person must be a U.S. citizen or lawful permanent resident, age 21+, with a B.S./B.A. in chemistry, pharmacology, or equivalent plus 4+ years drug manufacturing experience; (3) no misdemeanor or felony convictions; (4) manufacturers must furnish DOH with a list of all controlled substances manufactured or delivered within the state with NDC numbers; (5) manufacturers must electronically report all controlled substance sales in New York to DOH under 10 NYCRR 80.23(f).
- **effective_date**: Ongoing

#### NY State Compounding and Outsourcing Facility Requirements
- **regulation_key**: `ny_compounding_facility_requirements`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: New York
- **title**: NY State Compounding and Outsourcing Facility Requirements
- **description**: NY state pharmacy regulations impose specific compounding facility requirements beyond federal 503A/503B: (1) a dedicated manufacturing/compounding/dispensing area of no less than 100 square feet within the registered 300 sq ft minimum; (2) a refrigerator equipped with a thermometer maintaining 2-8 degrees Celsius at all times; (3) hot and cold running water in the compounding area; (4) adequate heating, ventilation, and lighting to safeguard drug purity/potency; (5) outsourcing facilities must be under the immediate supervision of a NY-licensed pharmacist; (6) the State Board of Pharmacy has inspection rights to enter any facility at reasonable times.

### glp_nonclinical

#### NY Wadsworth Center Clinical Laboratory Evaluation Program (CLEP) Permit
- **regulation_key**: `glp_21cfr58`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: New York
- **title**: NY Wadsworth Center Clinical Laboratory Evaluation Program (CLEP) Permit
- **description**: Under NY Public Health Law, ALL clinical and forensic laboratories and blood banks located in or accepting specimens from New York State must hold a NYS DOH clinical laboratory permit, administered by the Wadsworth Center CLEP. This goes well beyond federal CLIA: (1) the permit requirement applies to out-of-state facilities that accept clinical specimens collected in NY; (2) laboratory directors and assistant directors must hold a NYS Certificate of Qualification in the required categories; (3) an initial on-site survey is required before a permit is issued and before any specimen testing can begin; (4) proficiency testing must be maintained with passing scores in at least 2 of 3 consecutive tests; (5) individual test methodologies must receive NYS test approval before clinical use — NY is one of only two states (with Washington) that requires state-level test approval for laboratory-developed tests (LDTs); (6) application fee is $1,100 plus travel costs for out-of-state inspections. This is the most stringent state lab oversight program in the country.
- **effective_date**: Ongoing
- **source_url**: https://www.wadsworth.org/regulatory/clep/clinical-labs
- **source_name**: NYS DOH Wadsworth Center - Clinical Laboratory Evaluation Program
- **requires_written_policy**: false

#### NY Environmental Laboratory Approval Program (ELAP)
- **regulation_key**: `ny_environmental_lab_elap_certification`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: New York
- **title**: NY ELAP Environmental Laboratory Certification
- **description**: Under NY Public Health Law Section 502, the Wadsworth Center ELAP certifies all commercial, facility self-monitoring, and government-operated environmental laboratories performing analyses on samples originating from New York State. Fields include Air/Emissions, Drinking Water, Non-Potable Water, and Solid/Chemical Materials. Requirements: (1) laboratories must demonstrate proficiency for all requested analytes per Certification Manual Item 316; (2) continuing accreditation requires passing scores in at least 2 of 3 consecutive proficiency tests with study close dates 5-7 months apart; (3) at least one passing score must be less than 6 months old; (4) ELAP is an approved accrediting authority under NELAC/TNI standards.
- **effective_date**: 1984
- **source_url**: https://wadsworth.org/regulatory/elap
- **source_name**: NYS DOH Wadsworth Center - ELAP

### clinical_trials_gcp

#### NY Public Health Law Article 24-A — Protection of Human Subjects
- **regulation_key**: `ich_e6r2_gcp`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: New York
- **title**: NY Public Health Law Article 24-A - Protection of Human Subjects (Non-Federal Research)
- **description**: NY PHL Article 24-A (Sections 2440-2446) establishes state-level human subjects protections that apply to ALL human research conducted in New York that is NOT subject to federal regulations (non-federally-funded research, privately-sponsored trials not under an IND). Key requirements beyond federal: (1) voluntary informed consent must be subscribed to IN WRITING by the human subject — a statutory written consent mandate; (2) each institution conducting human research must establish a Human Research Review Committee (HRRC) of not fewer than 5 persons, approved by the NYS Commissioner of Health; (3) the HRRC must review each proposed project for necessity, adequate rights protection, risk-benefit balance, adequacy of informed consent methods, and competence/qualifications of investigators; (4) the Commissioner has authority to inspect records and enter facilities. Federally-funded research compliant with federal regulations is expressly exempt.
- **effective_date**: Ongoing
- **source_url**: https://law.justia.com/codes/new-york/pbh/article-24-a/
- **source_name**: NY Public Health Law Article 24-A
- **requires_written_policy**: true

#### NY Enhanced Informed Consent Standard (PHL 2805-d)
- **regulation_key**: `ind_application_21cfr312`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: New York
- **title**: NY Public Health Law 2805-d - Enhanced Informed Consent Standard
- **description**: NY PHL Section 2805-d establishes a state-specific informed consent standard applicable to all medical treatment and procedures including clinical trial interventions. The standard requires disclosure of alternatives and reasonably foreseeable risks and benefits as a reasonable practitioner would disclose. Unlike the federal standard focusing on regulatory compliance, NY law creates a private right of action for malpractice based on lack of informed consent for non-emergency treatment/procedures or diagnostic procedures involving bodily invasion. This creates additional liability exposure for clinical trial sites in New York beyond federal ICH-GCP informed consent requirements.
- **effective_date**: Ongoing
- **source_url**: https://www.nysenate.gov/legislation/laws/PBH/2805-D
- **source_name**: NY Public Health Law Section 2805-d
- **requires_written_policy**: false

### drug_supply_chain

#### NY State Board of Pharmacy Wholesale Drug Distributor Registration
- **regulation_key**: `wholesale_distribution_license`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: New York
- **title**: NY State Board of Pharmacy Wholesale Drug Distributor Registration
- **description**: Under NY Education Law Section 6808 and 8 NYCRR 63.6, no establishment may operate as a wholesaler of drugs/devices in New York without registration from the NY State Board of Pharmacy (NYSED). State-specific requirements beyond federal DSCSA: (1) a registered pharmacist or qualified supervisor (2+ years experience, bachelor's in chemistry/biochemistry/microbiology) must be present at all times; (2) the registered area must measure no less than 300 square feet, not shared with any other business; (3) 5-year record retention for all drug receipt and disposition; (4) out-of-state wholesalers must register ($825 fee) with notarized application and home state license verification; (5) an "isolated transaction" exemption exists for sales totaling less than $10,000 wholesale per calendar year into NY. Federal DSCSA preempts state pedigree/tracing requirements, but NY's establishment registration, personnel, and facility standards remain in effect.
- **effective_date**: Ongoing
- **source_url**: https://www.op.nysed.gov/professions/pharmacy-establishments/information/application-requirements-forms-in-state
- **source_name**: NYSED Office of Professions - Pharmacy Establishments
- **requires_written_policy**: false

### biosafety_lab

#### NY Biosafety Lab Oversight via CLEP and Institutional Requirements
- **regulation_key**: `bsl_classifications`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: New York
- **title**: NY Biosafety Lab Oversight via CLEP and Institutional Biosafety Committees
- **description**: New York does not have a standalone state biosafety laboratory registration program separate from federal BSL/CDC Select Agent requirements. However, state-specific layers include: (1) any lab handling clinical specimens must hold a CLEP permit, which includes on-site inspection of safety practices; (2) institutions in NY are required to maintain Institutional Biosafety Committees (IBCs) for oversight of research involving recombinant DNA and biological materials; (3) NY OSHA (PESH — Public Employee Safety and Health) applies state workplace safety standards to public sector labs that may exceed federal OSHA; (4) hazardous chemical handling falls under NY Environmental Conservation Law and Right-to-Know requirements.
- **effective_date**: Ongoing
- **source_url**: https://www.wadsworth.org/regulatory/clep
- **source_name**: NYS DOH Wadsworth Center
- **requires_written_policy**: false
