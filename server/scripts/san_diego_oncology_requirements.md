### USP <800> Hazardous Drugs Handling in Healthcare Settings
- **Title**: USP General Chapter <800> Hazardous Drug Handling
- **Regulation Key**: `usp_800_hazardous_drugs`
- **Rate Type**: healthcare
- **Citation**: USP General Chapter <800>; compendially applicable November 1, 2023
- **Description**: Antineoplastic HDs must be stored in negative-pressure areas with ≥12 air changes/hour. Closed System Drug Transfer Devices (CSTDs) required during administration of antineoplastic HDs. PPE including two pairs of chemo gloves, gown, and head/shoe covers required for compounding. Documented training required for all HD-handling staff. Assessment of Risk documentation required for any deviations.

- **Jurisdiction Name**: Federal (USP)

### IRB Review and Approval for FDA-Regulated Oncology Trials
- **Title**: IRB Review and Approval — FDA-Regulated Clinical Trials
- **Regulation Key**: `fda_irb_review`
- **Rate Type**: healthcare
- **Citation**: 21 CFR Part 56 (FDA — Institutional Review Boards)
- **Description**: All clinical investigations involving investigational drugs or biologics (including oncology trials) must be reviewed and approved by an FDA-registered IRB before initiation. IRB must conduct continuing review at least annually. Full board review required for greater-than-minimal-risk research (standard for oncology trials). IRB records must be retained ≥3 years after research completion.
- **Current Value**: FDA-registered IRB approval required before trial initiation; annual continuing review
- **Jurisdiction Level**: federal
- **Jurisdiction Name**: Federal (FDA)

- **Effective Date**: 2020-01-20

### California Experimental Subjects Bill of Rights
- **Title**: California Experimental Subjects Bill of Rights
- **Regulation Key**: `ca_experimental_subjects_bill_of_rights`
- **Rate Type**: healthcare
- **Citation**: California HSC §24172 and §24173
- **Description**: All investigators conducting a medical experiment in California (including oncology clinical trials) must offer each participant a copy of the Experimental Subjects Bill of Rights in a language the subject is fluent in. The document must inform subjects of their rights to be told the nature/purpose of the experiment, procedures, risks, benefits, alternatives, and the right to withdraw at any time without prejudice. Must be signed and dated by the subject or guardian.
- **Current Value**: Written Bill of Rights must be provided and signed before enrollment; available in subject's language
- **Jurisdiction Level**: state
- **Jurisdiction Name**: California

- **Effective Date**: null

- **Title**: Hospital Patient Rights — Informed Consent and Research Disclosure (CCR Title 22 §70707)
- **Regulation Key**: `ca_hospital_patient_rights`

Hospitals must have a documented Patient Rights policy and provide it to patients.
