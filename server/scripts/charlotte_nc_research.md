## General Labor

### minimum_wage

*NC minimum wage matches the federal rate ($7.25/hr). The state has its own Wage and Hour Act (N.C.G.S. 95-25.3) but does not set a rate above the federal baseline. Tipped minimum wage also matches federal ($2.13/hr). Documenting below because the state statute establishes employer obligations around tip credit notification.*

### overtime

*Federal only — no state-specific requirements beyond FLSA. NC Wage and Hour Act (N.C.G.S. 95-25.4) mirrors FLSA 1.5x after 40 hours/week. No daily overtime, no double time, no seventh-day overtime. The state law primarily extends FLSA-like coverage to employees at smaller employers not subject to federal FLSA.*

### sick_leave

*Federal only — no state-specific requirements. North Carolina has no mandatory paid or unpaid sick leave law for private sector employers. Senate Bill 622 (2025-2026 session) proposed paid sick leave but has not been enacted.*

## Healthcare

### hipaa_privacy

#### State Health Privacy Laws
- **regulation_key**: `state_health_privacy_laws`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: North Carolina
- **title**: North Carolina Health Privacy Protections Beyond HIPAA
- **description**: NC has multiple layers of health privacy protection exceeding HIPAA. (1) Physician-Patient Privilege (N.C.G.S. § 8-53): Communications between patients and physicians are statutorily privileged — broader than HIPAA in creating an evidentiary privilege preventing compelled disclosure in court proceedings. The privilege belongs to the patient, not the physician. A judge may compel disclosure only if necessary to proper administration of justice. Nurse-patient privilege separately established under N.C.G.S. § 8-53.13. (2) Mental Health/Substance Abuse Confidentiality (N.C.G.S. §§ 122C-52 through 122C-56): Stricter than HIPAA for mental health, developmental disabilities, and substance abuse records. Unauthorized disclosure is a Class 3 misdemeanor punishable by fine up to $500. Narrow exceptions for disclosure. (3) NC courts have reaffirmed a common law duty of confidentiality for ALL healthcare entities, independent of HIPAA. (4) Data Breach Notification (N.C.G.S. § 75-65, Identity Theft Protection Act): Applies to ALL businesses (not just HIPAA covered entities). Must notify the NC Attorney General's Consumer Protection Division. Notice must include description of protective actions, business phone number, credit monitoring advice, and consumer reporting agency contact info. Substitute notice threshold: cost exceeding $250,000 or more than 500,000 affected persons.
- **source_url**: https://www.ncleg.net/enactedlegislation/statutes/html/bysection/chapter_8/gs_8-53.html
- **source_name**: N.C. Gen. Stat. §§ 8-53, 122C-52–56, 75-65
- **requires_written_policy**: true
