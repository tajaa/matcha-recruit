## Manufacturing

### process_safety

#### OSHA Process Safety Management (State Plan Adoption)
- **regulation_key**: `osha_psm`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: North Carolina
- **title**: NC OSHA Process Safety Management
- **description**: North Carolina operates an OSHA-approved State Plan and adopts federal OSHA standards verbatim, including 29 CFR 1910.119 (PSM). Applies to facilities with highly hazardous chemicals above threshold quantities. Requires written PSM program, process hazard analysis, operating procedures, training, management of change, pre-startup safety review, mechanical integrity, and emergency planning. NC DOL enforces with state-specific penalties.
- **current_value**: Federal PSM adopted verbatim — applies to HHC processes above threshold quantities
- **source_url**: https://www.osha.gov/stateplans/nc
- **source_name**: OSHA State Plans - North Carolina
- **requires_written_policy**: true

#### Process Hazard Analysis

- **jurisdiction_name**: North Carolina
- **title**: Process Hazard Analysis Requirements

### environmental_compliance

- **jurisdiction_name**: North Carolina

#### NESHAP Compliance (Rubber Tire Manufacturing)
- **regulation_key**: `neshap_compliance`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: North Carolina
- **title**: NESHAP Subpart XXXX — Rubber Tire Manufacturing
- **description**: National Emission Standards for Hazardous Air Pollutants for rubber tire manufacturing (40 CFR 63 Subpart XXXX). Regulates HAP emissions including hexane, toluene, formaldehyde, styrene, and methanol. THC standard of 64 g/Mg rubber processed on 15-day rolling average. Requires continuous THC monitoring (CEMS), bag leak detection on fabric filters, and electronic reporting via CEDRI. - **current_value**: THC limit 64 g/Mg rubber processed (15-day rolling average), CEMS required
- **source_url**: https://www.epa.gov/stationary-sources-air-pollution/rubber-tire-manufacturing-national-emission-standards-hazardous

### chemical_safety

#### NC Hazardous Chemicals Right to Know Act
- **regulation_key**: `right_to_know`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: North Carolina
- **title**: NC Hazardous Chemicals Right to Know Act
- **description**: North Carolina Right to Know Act requires facilities to provide a written list of hazardous substances to the local fire department. Reporting thresholds are lower than federal EPCRA: 55 gallons or 500 pounds (vs federal 10,000 lbs for non-EHS). This is a state-specific requirement that goes beyond the federal EPCRA Tier II threshold.

#### EPCRA Tier II Chemical Inventory Reporting
- **regulation_key**: `chemical_inventory_reporting`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: North Carolina
- **title**: EPCRA Tier II Reporting (NC Implementation)
- **description**: Facilities storing hazardous chemicals above threshold quantities (500 lbs or TPQ for EHS, 10,000 lbs for others) must submit annual Tier II reports via E-Plan from January 1 to March 1. NC SERC and most LEPCs only accept submissions through E-Plan. Filing fees apply based on number of chemicals reported. 2025 federal update aligned EPCRA hazard categories with 2024 OSHA HCS GHS hazard classes.
- **current_value**: Annual Tier II report via E-Plan by March 1; filing fees apply
- **source_url**: https://www.ncdps.gov/our-organization/emergency-management/hazardous-materials/chemical-safety-reporting

- **title**: Machine Guarding Requirements (Subpart O)

### labor_relations

#### Right to Work
- **regulation_key**: `right_to_work`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: North Carolina
- **title**: North Carolina Right to Work Law
- **description**: North Carolina is a right-to-work state under N.C. General Statutes Chapter 95, Article 10. Employees cannot be required to join a union or pay union dues as a condition of employment. Employers cannot discriminate against employees for joining or refusing to join a union. Public sector collective bargaining is prohibited by statute. 2025 legislative session includes proposals to both constitutionalize right-to-work protections and repeal them, neither enacted.
- **current_value**: Right-to-work state — no mandatory union membership or dues
- **source_url**: https://www.ncleg.gov/EnactedLegislation/Statutes/PDF/ByArticle/Chapter_95/Article_10.pdf
- **source_name**: NC General Statutes Chapter 95 Article 10
- **requires_written_policy**: false

## General Labor

### minimum_wage

*North Carolina follows the federal minimum wage of $7.25/hour. No state minimum wage above federal. Proposed legislation (S 326 in 2025-2026 session) to increase to $22/hour has not been enacted.*

### overtime

*Federal FLSA overtime applies — 1.5x after 40 hours/week. No state-specific overtime rules beyond federal (no daily overtime, no double time). NC does not have state-specific exempt salary thresholds above the federal level.*

### sick_leave

*No state-mandated paid sick leave. North Carolina does not require private employers to provide paid or unpaid sick leave beyond FMLA protections.*

### leave

*No state paid family leave program. Workers rely on federal FMLA (50+ employees, 12 weeks unpaid). Proposed PFML bills (SB 480, HB 499 in 2025-2026) have not been enacted.*

### meal_breaks

*No state-mandated meal or rest breaks for adult employees. Only requirement: breaks of less than 30 minutes must be compensated.*

### anti_discrimination

*Federal Title VII, ADA, ADEA, and GINA apply. North Carolina does not have a comprehensive state employment anti-discrimination statute for private employers. No state salary history ban, no state pay transparency law, no state mandatory harassment prevention training requirement.*

### minor_work_permit

*North Carolina has state-specific child labor laws under N.C.G.S. Chapter 95. Minors under 18 require youth employment certificates. Prohibited hazardous occupations for minors include manufacturing with power-driven machinery.*
