### gmp_manufacturing

#### Massachusetts Controlled Substances Registration (MCSR) for Drug Manufacturers
- **regulation_key**: `cgmp_drugs_21cfr210_211`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: Massachusetts
- **title**: Massachusetts Controlled Substances Registration (MCSR) for Drug Manufacturers
- **description**: Under M.G.L. c. 94C, Section 7 and 105 CMR 700.000, any entity that manufactures, distributes, or dispenses controlled substances in Massachusetts must obtain a Massachusetts Controlled Substances Registration (MCSR) from the Department of Public Health, in addition to federal DEA registration. Critically, Massachusetts defines "controlled substance" to include ALL prescription pharmaceuticals (Schedules I-VI), not just federally scheduled drugs. Schedule VI covers prescription "legend" drugs not in federal Schedules I-V. This means MA manufacturer registration requirements are broader than federal DEA registration, covering essentially every prescription drug manufactured in the state.
- **effective_date**: Ongoing
- **source_url**: https://www.mass.gov/info-details/manufacturer-and-distributor-registration-information
- **source_name**: Massachusetts DPH - Manufacturer and Distributor Registration
- **requires_written_policy**: false

#### Massachusetts Sterile Compounding Pharmacy Licensure (247 CMR 17.00)
- **regulation_key**: `fda_facility_registration`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: Massachusetts
- **title**: Massachusetts Sterile Compounding Pharmacy Licensure (247 CMR 17.00)
- **description**: Massachusetts requires a separate state license for sterile compounding under 247 CMR 17.00, with requirements beyond federal cGMP and USP 797. Key state-specific provisions: (1) annual on-site inspection by Board staff is mandatory; (2) supervising pharmacist must be physically on-site at each sterile compounding area; (3) SECs may not have more than one door to adjacent areas, and doors must use interlocking design preventing simultaneous opening; (4) refrigerators are prohibited inside ISO-classified SECs; (5) floor drains are prohibited in SECs; (6) a formal written Quality Assurance Program per USP 1163 is required; (7) institutional sterile compounders must obtain a separate institutional sterile compounding pharmacy license (applications required as of January 2025). Derived from M.G.L. c. 112, Sections 39G, 39I, and 39J.
- **effective_date**: 2014 (original); institutional license requirement effective 2025-01-01
- **source_url**: https://www.mass.gov/doc/247-cmr-1700-sterile-compounding/download
- **source_name**: MA Board of Registration in Pharmacy - 247 CMR 17.00
- **requires_written_policy**: true

### glp_nonclinical

#### Massachusetts Clinical Laboratory License and Certificate of Approval (105 CMR 180.000)
- **regulation_key**: `glp_21cfr58`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: Massachusetts
- **title**: Massachusetts Clinical Laboratory License and Certificate of Approval (105 CMR 180.000)
- **description**: Under M.G.L. c. 111D and implementing regulation 105 CMR 180.000, Massachusetts requires state licensure or certificate of approval for all clinical laboratories, beyond federal CLIA certification. Hospital/clinic-controlled labs need a Certificate of Approval (Limited or Full class, renewable every 2 years). All other clinical labs need a state license (also 2-year term). State-specific requirements include: (1) pre-licensure enrollment in an approved proficiency-testing program covering all analytes performed; (2) lab director qualifications defined by state standards (must be certified by American Board of Pathology or equivalent national accrediting board acceptable to DPH); (3) technologist qualification standards under 105 CMR 180.155; (4) state proficiency testing results must be authorized for release to the MA Clinical Laboratory Program.
- **effective_date**: Ongoing
- **source_url**: https://www.mass.gov/regulations/105-CMR-18000-the-operation-approval-and-licensing-of-clinical-laboratories
- **source_name**: MA DPH - 105 CMR 180.00 Clinical Laboratory Licensing
- **requires_written_policy**: false

### clinical_trials_gcp

#### Massachusetts Clinical Trial Insurance Coverage Mandate
- **regulation_key**: `ich_e6r2_gcp`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: Massachusetts
- **title**: Massachusetts Clinical Trial Insurance Coverage Mandate (M.G.L. c. 175, Section 110L)
- **description**: Massachusetts mandates that all insurance policies issued, delivered, or renewed in the Commonwealth must cover and reimburse patient care services provided during a qualified clinical trial to the same extent as if the patient were not in a trial. This goes beyond federal requirements by: (1) applying to all insurers in the state (not just those under the ACA); (2) requiring coverage for routine care costs associated with clinical trials; (3) establishing specific state-level standards for qualifying IRBs — an IRB qualifies only if it meets all federal CFR requirements, has not been disqualified by NIH/FDA, and has rectified any noncompliance within the past 3 years; (4) prohibiting insurers from denying coverage solely because a patient is enrolled in a clinical trial. Excludes: costs reimbursed by trial sponsors, services inconsistent with national standards of care, and services provided at greater frequency/intensity primarily for trial needs.
- **effective_date**: 2012
- **source_url**: https://malegislature.gov/Laws/GeneralLaws/PartI/TitleXXII/Chapter175/Section110L
- **source_name**: Massachusetts General Laws - Chapter 175, Section 110L
- **requires_written_policy**: false

### drug_supply_chain

#### Massachusetts Wholesale Drug Distributor License (247 CMR 7.00)
- **regulation_key**: `wholesale_distribution_license`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: Massachusetts
- **title**: Massachusetts Wholesale Drug Distributor License (247 CMR 7.00)
- **description**: Under 247 CMR 7.00, Massachusetts requires all wholesale drug distributors located in or distributing into the Commonwealth to obtain a state license from the Board of Registration in Pharmacy. State-specific requirements beyond federal DSCSA include: (1) application under oath before a notary public; (2) disclosure of all trade/business names, all facility addresses with contact persons for storage, handling, and distribution; (3) personnel competency requirements — each employee involved in wholesale distribution must have education, training, and experience sufficient to maintain drug quality, safety, and security; (4) detailed record-keeping of all transactions including source identity, drug identity/quantity, and dates of receipt/distribution; (5) compliance with minimum standards for storage and handling under 247 CMR 7.04. Federal DSCSA preempts state pedigree/tracing requirements, but MA's licensing and personnel standards remain in effect.
- **effective_date**: Ongoing
- **source_url**: https://www.mass.gov/doc/247-cmr-7-wholesale-druggists/download
- **source_name**: MA Board of Registration in Pharmacy - 247 CMR 7.00
- **requires_written_policy**: false

### sunshine_open_payments

#### Massachusetts Pharmaceutical Marketing Code of Conduct and Gift Ban
- **regulation_key**: `state_gift_ban_laws`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: Massachusetts
- **title**: Massachusetts Pharmaceutical Marketing Code of Conduct and Gift Ban (M.G.L. c. 111N / 105 CMR 970.000)
- **description**: Massachusetts has one of the strictest pharmaceutical gift ban laws in the nation, enacted under M.G.L. Chapter 111N (Chapter 305 of the Acts of 2008) with implementing regulations at 105 CMR 970.000. Key state-specific requirements beyond the federal Sunshine Act: (1) ANNUAL REGISTRATION: Every pharmaceutical/medical device manufacturer engaging in sales and marketing in MA must register annually with DPH and pay a $2,000 annual registration fee; (2) GIFT BAN: Entertainment and recreational items of ANY value are prohibited (tickets, sporting events, concerts, sporting equipment, leisure/vacation trips); (3) MEAL RESTRICTIONS: Meals to healthcare practitioners are permitted only if of "modest value," provided during educational/informational presentations about drug benefits/risks, and in a setting conducive to informational communication; (4) DISCLOSURE: Companies must annually disclose (by July 1) the value, nature, purpose, and specific recipient of any fee, payment, subsidy, or economic benefit of $50 or more provided to any covered recipient; (5) NONCOMPLIANCE REPORTING: Manufacturers must self-report all incidents of noncompliance to both DPH and the Office of the Attorney General; (6) COVERED RECIPIENTS broader than federal — includes anyone authorized to prescribe, dispense, or purchase, not just physicians and teaching hospitals.
- **effective_date**: 2009-07-01 (original); 2012-11-26 (amended regulations)
- **source_url**: https://www.mass.gov/doc/105-cmr-970-pharmaceutical-and-medical-device-manufacturer-conduct/download
- **source_name**: MA DPH - 105 CMR 970.000 Pharmaceutical and Medical Device Manufacturer Conduct
- **requires_written_policy**: true

### biosafety_lab

#### Massachusetts Biological Waste Management (105 CMR 480.000)
- **regulation_key**: `chemical_hygiene_plan`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: Massachusetts
- **title**: Massachusetts Minimum Requirements for Medical or Biological Waste Management (105 CMR 480.000)
- **description**: Under 105 CMR 480.000, Massachusetts establishes state-specific requirements for storage, treatment, disposal, and transportation of medical or biological waste that go beyond federal OSHA requirements. Key provisions: (1) biological waste is broadly defined to include blood/blood products, pathological waste, cultures/stocks of infectious agents, contaminated animal carcasses/bedding, sharps, AND biotechnology by-product effluents including recombinant DNA; (2) record-keeping log with secured, consecutively numbered pages must be maintained for all biological waste treated on-site or shipped off-site, retained for 3 years; (3) approved disinfection methods must be validated using recording thermometers, thermocouples, parametric monitoring devices, thermal indicator strips, or DPH-approved equivalents; (4) solid biological waste must be accumulated in red-bag-lined containers; (5) the inclusion of rDNA biotechnology effluents as regulated biological waste is a state-specific addition beyond federal baseline.
- **effective_date**: Ongoing
- **source_url**: https://www.mass.gov/regulations/105-CMR-48000-minimum-requirements-for-the-management-of-medical-or-biological-waste-state-sanitary-code-chapter-viii
- **source_name**: MA DPH - 105 CMR 480.000 Biological Waste Management
- **requires_written_policy**: false

#### Massachusetts DPH BSL-3 Laboratory Inspection Authority
- **regulation_key**: `bsl_classifications`
- **jurisdiction_level**: state
- **jurisdiction_name**: Massachusetts
- **title**: Massachusetts DPH BSL-3 Laboratory Inspection Authority
- **description**: The Massachusetts Department of Public Health conducts regular inspections of BSL-3 laboratory spaces statewide. While much of the detailed BSL-3/BSL-4 permitting is handled at the local level (notably Boston's robust permitting system under BPHC requiring annual operating permits for BSL-3+ labs and BSL-2+ labs working with rDNA), the state DPH maintains inspection authority over BSL-3 facilities throughout Massachusetts. Massachusetts does not have a comprehensive statewide BSL registration/permitting statute comparable to Boston's local regulations; the state role is primarily through DPH inspection authority and the biological waste regulations under 105 CMR 480.
- **effective_date**: Ongoing
- **source_url**: https://www.mass.gov/lists/clinical-laboratory-program-laws-regulations-and-circular-letters
- **source_name**: MA DPH - Clinical Laboratory Program
- **requires_written_policy**: false
