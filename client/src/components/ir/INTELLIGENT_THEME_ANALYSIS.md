# Intelligent Theme Analysis

Matcha's branded name for the AI-powered incident analysis engine in the IR surface.

## What it does

Intelligent Theme Analysis processes incident reports and surfaces:

- **Categorization** — automatically classifies incidents (behavioral, safety, policy, etc.) from free-text descriptions, without requiring reporters to pre-select a type
- **Root Cause** — infers underlying systemic causes from incident narrative and prior incident history at the location
- **Recommendations** — generates corrective actions and policy steps mapped to your company handbook and applicable jurisdiction requirements
- **Theme Clusters** — identifies recurring patterns across incidents over rolling time windows, flagging risk clusters before they compound into larger issues

## Why this is different from standard IR systems

| Standard IR | Intelligent Theme Analysis |
|---|---|
| Manual categorization by the reporting manager | Automated from free-text at submission — no post-hoc tagging required |
| Severity set by human judgment on a single incident | Severity scored against behavioral patterns, policy context, and incident history |
| Incidents are siloed — each record is independent | Cross-incident pattern detection: clusters and themes surface across records |
| Root cause requires manual investigation | Root cause inferred from narrative + historical signals |
| Policy tie-in is a manual lookup | Categorization is mapped to handbook policy entries automatically |
| Recommendations are ad hoc | Recommendations are jurisdiction-aware and grounded in your actual policies |

## Key differentiators for user communication

1. **Proactive cluster detection** — most IR systems tell you what happened. Intelligent Theme Analysis tells you what keeps happening and where.
2. **Policy-mapped output** — categorization doesn't just label an incident; it links to the relevant policy section in your handbook so managers have context for the conversation.
3. **No tagging burden on reporters** — reporters describe what happened in plain language. The analysis handles classification, so the system gets better data without asking reporters to become compliance experts.
4. **Root cause, not just record** — the root cause panel draws on incident history at the location and across the company, not just the current description, so it can surface management or process failures that a single-incident view would miss.
